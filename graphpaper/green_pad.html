Q: What is Ohm's Law and how is it used in electrical circuits?
A: Ohm's Law states that the current (I) through a conductor between two points is directly proportional to the voltage (V) across the two points and inversely proportional to the resistance (R). It is expressed as V = IR. This law is used to calculate the voltage, current, or resistance in a circuit when the other two quantities are known.

Q: What is the difference between series and parallel circuits?
A: In a series circuit, components are connected end-to-end so that the same current flows through each component. In a parallel circuit, components are connected across the same two points, so the voltage across each component is the same, but the current can vary. Series circuits have one path for current, while parallel circuits have multiple paths.

Q: How does a capacitor store energy in an electrical system?
A: A capacitor stores energy in the form of an electric field between its plates. When a voltage is applied, charge accumulates on the plates, creating a potential difference. The energy stored is given by the formula E = ½CV², where C is the capacitance and V is the voltage.

Q: What is the function of a transformer in an electrical system?
A: A transformer changes the voltage level in an AC electrical system. It consists of two coils (primary and secondary) wound around a core. By electromagnetic induction, it can step up (increase) or step down (decrease) the voltage depending on the ratio of turns in the coils.
